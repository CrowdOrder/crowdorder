The Ultimate Start Up Business Checklist

  • ben.mcaskell@crowdorder.co.uk
  • 22 Sep 2020

Starting a new business is one of the most exciting things a budding entrepreneur can do, but let’s face it, if you’re new to it, it can be a lot to think about. We wanted to make starting your own business easier with an ultimate checklist for start up businesses and to make sure you get off on the right foot.

You’ve got this amazing idea for a business, but at this stage it is just that, an idea. There is a ton to consider when starting your own business ranging from skills, personnel, business planning and finances and it can be a minefield.

This guide will get you through the early stages of your new business and ensure you are set up for success. So, let’s get started and get you on the road to becoming a successful business owner with our Ultimate Start Up Business Checklist.

Drill down on your Unique Selling Point (USP)

Your new business needs to have a unique selling point or a USP in order to stand out. What are you doing differently to your competition? It would be ideal if you could sum up your USP in one sentence and be able to use this when pitching your business to new customers or even investors.

It doesn’t need to be super clever, but it needs to show people who you are. One of my favourite examples of this is for car manufacturer Volvo.

Volvo for Life

Their slogan “Volvo for Life” is reminding people that safety is their USP and what they are known best for.

Find a Business Mentor

Now, this is easier said than done. If you can find someone with business experience that can guide you, it is worth its weight in gold. This may be someone who has experience within the same industry as your business or just someone who has more experience in areas of business that you don’t. For example, if your skills are in operations and fulfilment, you may want a mentor who understands finances or marketing. It’s important that a mentor can help you see the wider picture and also help fill your skills gaps with expert knowledge.

Create a Business Plan

When starting a business, you need to have a clear idea of where you want to be 6 months, one year and even five years down the line. You need to consider what targets you want to hit, how you are going to do it. This is not only important for your own clarity but helps you be accountable to yourself when growing your business. It’s also essential should you think you might want investment in the future.

Register Web Domains and Trademarks

Having visibility online is now essential, not a nice to have. When you have chosen the name of your business, make sure a suitable domain name is available at a reasonable price. Building a website might not be needed straight away but being safe in the knowledge that you own the domain name is better than having to change the name of your business or a poor domain name.

If you think your business might need some trademarks, make sure you get in touch with a lawyer.

Set up your Business Structure

If you have partners, ensure you set up your business structure correctly. If you own a business 50/50 with someone, this shouldn’t be difficult, but it may be worth working with an accountant to set this up correctly if shares in the business aren’t equal.

Calculate Profitability of your Business

The main aim of starting a business is to generate a profit. You need to create a forecast of when you think your business will become profitable and projections for the future. There are a ton of financial and accounting software and services out there, so be sure to look into this.

Set up a Business Bank Account

I cannot stress this enough, do not use your personal bank account for business use. You need to keep your personal and business finances separate not only for ease of tracking, but you will get extra overdraft facilities to help with your cashflow.

Arrange Business Insurance

Every business needs insurance. You do not want to pay for damages and liability out of your own pocket so a small monthly fee to protect your business is highly recommended.

Register for Taxes

You’ll need to register for taxes as soon as you start the business. You can do this with HMRC for Corporation Tax or Self-Assessment if sole trading. Once you start employing people you will also need to register for VAT and PAYE so you can pay them.

Create your Website

You’ve already bought the domain name and now you should be looking to get a website live. You can use some out-of-the-box solutions like Squarespace, WordPress and Wix if you want to build the site yourself as these require no coding or design experience.

If you want something slightly more complicated or bespoke, I would recommend using a specialist Web Design and Development agency to get a site built tailored to your needs.

Create Social Media Accounts

Some businesses will use social media more than others and use the platforms in different ways but its still important to have some sort of social presence as consumers often check Facebook, Linkedin and Instagram pages before using a service or buying a product. Different businesses will find better traction on different social media networks, but this is something you can test as your business grows. It might even be worth working with a Social Media agency to manage your social media campaigns.

Ask Friends and Family to Promote you Online

Friends and family should be the biggest advocates for your business right at the start, so why not ask them to share your website or social media profile for your new business to all their networks? It may lead to a few sales or leads and get you some traction in the early days

Look into the Technology you may need

You will need to think about what technology your business needs. This may be physical hardware like laptops and phones but can also be tools that make running your business easier. We partner with lots of companies that offer technology services that can help your business.

Choose your Business Applications

There’s a number of technology stacks out there that cover email, calendars, communications and project management such as Google for Business and Microsoft 365. Both use the Cloud so you can work from any device meaning they are both great for collaboration. If you need more specialist apps, you may need to research this further.

Keep your data safe

Companies that lose data also lose business. Use cloud-based software for peace of mind.
