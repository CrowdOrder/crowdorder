How to Choose the Best CRM for a Startup: A Step-by-step Guide

  • ben.mcaskell@crowdorder.co.uk
  • 22 Sep 2020

Startups and small businesses often need to scale at pace and can often struggle to streamline their leads and data management. It can be easy to just use an Excel file or Google Doc, which may work fine if you have a couple of leads a week, but not if you want to scale your business rapidly and manage tens or hundreds of leads a week. You’ll end up mismanaging communications with customers and getting a ton of bounces on your email marketing.

This article will give you an overview of how to choose the best CRM for a startup and how to manage your workflow and customer data efficiently.

3 Major Benefits of a CRM for Startups and Small Businesses

When starting a business, you need to consider the collaboration between your team or teams. A CRM should manage all communications with customers and leads and help to identify new business opportunities.

Solidify Client Relationships

Dealing with customers and potential clients is made so much easier with a decent CRM. You can group all your communications with prospects and ensure you are reminded when to follow up on various projects and comms. The CRM will show the history of your communication with all companies, so you know what to discuss and you don’t have to go in cold. You whole team can see this to ensure time is used wisely and customer experience is as smooth as possible.

Organize Your Sales Data

CRM platforms are great for organizing all customer data and notes about clients. In order to get the most from your CRM system, you need to evaluate your current sales processes and what you want to improve. You may find that the CRM can fill those gaps that you require.

The benefits of the CRM for sales are:

  • Keeping records of all your previous conversations and interactions.
  • The option to customize your clients’ contacts into various logical groups that help you tailor follow-ups easily
  • The CRM contains all the conversation details that are necessary for business owners to manage and foster good relationships with clients

Accelerate Startup Growth and Sales

A good CRM can really help a startup with rapid growth. It allows you to visualize your sales pipeline, boost productivity and give your sales team to do more of what they do best, sell!

A CRM can influence your revenue greatly and make it easier to upsell and keep in contact with customers and prospects alike.

What to Consider When Choosing the Best CRM for Startups and Small Businesses

There is a lot of choice when it comes to choosing the right CRM for your businesses, but it is important to consider the following when looking for a CRM platform:

Scalability

Your business won’t be small forever, so it’s important to choose a CRM that can grow and evolve with the needs of your business. It’s now good spending all your time and effort integrating a CRM into your day-to-day, only to change it a few years down the line when it doesn’t have the scale and functionality your business needs.

You should have an idea of what you want a CRM to do and you should go for the platform that does those things the best. Don’t go for a all singing and dancing solution where you wont use half of the features. It’s a waste of money and can dilute what the CRM should be used for.

The CRM should be flexible enough to adapt to your current business needs and its functionality must grow as your startup does.

Tracking of sales

A decent CRM should at the very least allow you to store contacts, allow you to collaborate with all members of your sales and marketing team, and manage all relationships and leads.

Ensuring all your sales team are on the same page when it comes to prospect and client communications is extremely important with a business that is scaling at a rapid rate. It helps save time and customer frustration and means workflows can be picked up by anyone in the team when on holiday or out of the office.

Client management

CRM platforms can be useful with managing contacts and accessing their information. It’s also great to give certain members of staff different access and restrictions for certain high profile or sensitive clients.

Regular Updates

CRM’s are always evolving, and you will find regular updates and new features cropping up that may improve your productivity further.

Reporting Capabilities

Your CRM should enable you to create user-friendly and customizable performance reports to show what is happening at every stage of the marketing funnel. Your reporting metrics should include:

  • Sales stage (customized stage of your sales cycle)
  • Type of lead
  • Source of lead (Email marketing campaign, Google ads, Facebook ads etc…)
  • Sales profits, etc.
  • The ability to dive deeper into your analytics may cost a penny in some CRMs, so be aware when evaluating one.
A pipeline view is basically a breakdown of every single stage in your customer acquisition pipeline. This pipeline view usually shows the number of leads in each stage and gives an option to drill down to certain leads. The CRM should give the business owner the freedom of creating custom stages with respect to their sales cycles and move leads through the funnel as their users are drawing closer to the purchase.

Email Integration

With email integration, you won’t need to switch between your CRM and email making it easier to find previous interactions and record communications seamlessly.

Security

Security is of the upmost importance when choosing a CRM for your start up or small business. CRM’s can track anyone who is on the CRM in terms of the contacts they are looking at and what changes they are making to profiles. If any errors are coming up, CRM’s can often pick this up too.
